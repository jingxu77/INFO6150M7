Glaucoma

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is an eye disease that damages the optic nerve, often due to high pressure in the eye, and can lead to blindness if not treated. It's more common in older adults and can run in families.

Causes

Glaucoma usually happens when eye fluid doesn't flow out properly, increasing eye pressure. Sometimes, the cause is unknown, but it can be genetic or due to eye injury, infection, or surgery.

Symptoms

Glaucoma is particularly challenging because it often does not present early symptoms.

  • No early symptoms in many cases
  • Open-angle glaucoma leads to gradual peripheral vision loss
  • Angle-closure glaucoma causes sudden severe eye pain, redness, vision loss, and seeing halos

Diagnosis

Doctors use various tests like eye exams, pressure measurements, and imaging to check for glaucoma.

Treatment

Treatment for glaucoma aims to lower eye pressure and includes medications, laser treatment, or surgery.

  • Eye drops or pills
  • Laser treatment
  • Surgical options
  • Regular monitoring is essential

Living with Glaucoma

Living with glaucoma involves lifestyle adjustments and adherence to treatment to manage the condition effectively.

  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy diet
  • Following medication schedules
  • Avoid smoking and limit caffeine
  • Protect eyes from injury
  • Modify certain activities, like some yoga poses, to avoid pressure increases
